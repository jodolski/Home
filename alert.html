Project: Convo Alert

May 8th, 2020

Introduction

Convo Alert is an emergency broadcasting system that communicates in a way that is understood by all individuals including the Deaf and Blind. Convo Alert is Convo Communication's first independently developed cloud product (not funded by the FCC) that aims to deliver safety in schools and businesses across the world. Convo Alert also has a strategic role in growing VRS (video relay services) customers through the exposure of Convo Alert. As product manager of Convo Alert, I took it from ideation to launch. Here are the steps taken.

Pain Points

  • Existing systems are very expensive.
  • Existing systems require significant amount of maintenance.
  • Existing systems are largely hardware-based.

Process

  • Conducted interviews with current customers to discover pain points.
  • Documented personas, current system workflows, and current costs.
  • Developed goals (e.g., reduce cost, low maintenance, supports integrations).
  • Ran SWOT and competitive analysis.
  • Collaborated with UX researcher to develop user workflows and mockups.
  • Presented with UX researcher several prototypes to customers.
  • Proposed to executive and engineering leadership for support.
  • Validated first MVP to be run in production on AWS.
  • Presented demo to internal company and gathered feedback.
  • Coordinated with marketing and sales for launch preparedness.
  • Validated final version to be launched.

Product Benefits

  • Little to no cost to implement.
  • All maintenance is run by Convo's cloud engineers.
  • Zero computing maintenance required.
  • Vibrant and clear alert messages and animations.
  • Future support for haptics feedback integration.
  • Robust administration features (i.e., prevent unauthorized users).
  • Easy alert template development workflow.
  • Simple sign up / registration for students and parents.
  • Support for multiple channels for multiple locations.
  • Support for automated local emergency service requests.
  • Support for integration with existing emergency broadcasting products.

Results
